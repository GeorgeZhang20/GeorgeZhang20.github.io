May 2026 — Present Houston, TX LLM Systems

Interpretable, Embedding-Free LLM Router

Co-first author Prof. Jiarong Xing · Rice University
  • Building a rule-based router that selects the answering model from readable lexical and structural features via an LLM-in-the-loop rule-evolution loop — no neural encoder on the routing path.
  • Showed surface and lexical features match dense-embedding routers on all five routing benchmarks, recasting the assumed "semantic wall" as a weak-feature-extractor artifact rather than a fundamental limit.
  • On EmbedLLM, 7 human-readable rules reach 0.662 test accuracy — beating a tuned embedding-KNN router (0.652), the single best model (0.607), and 7 of 8 neural encoders.
May 2026 — Present Houston, TX LLM Systems

Multi-Turn LLM Routing with Reasoning-Effort Control

Co-first author Prof. Jiarong Xing · Rice University
  • Building toward a unified router that jointly controls reasoning effort and model choice across the turns of an agentic task — separating compute-bound failures (more thinking suffices) from capability-bound ones (a stronger model is needed).
  • Grounding the design in an offline (model × effort) oracle mapping accuracy/cost frontiers, with a decision-regime decomposition (raise-effort vs. must-switch) and a source-verified survey establishing the open gap.

The Routing Plateau: Limits of LLM Routers

NeurIPS 2026 submission · Prof. Jiarong Xing · Rice University
  • Benchmarked 21 routing methods (classifier, retrieval, pairwise-ranking, IRT, contrastive, cascade) across five benchmarks and six encoders under one protocol, revealing a consistent "routing plateau" 10–30 points below the per-query oracle.
  • Traced the plateau to a correctness-prediction bottleneck via hard-query stratification, oracle-gap attribution, and pairwise error-exchange analysis.
  • Built a new 300K-query benchmark (2.8M query/model correctness labels, per-call cost, nine commercial LLMs); data scaling, stronger encoders, and fine-tuning recover up to 14.6% of the oracle gap.
Feb 2025 — May 2026 Houston, TX LLM Systems

Efficiency Methods for LLM Multi-Agent Systems

ACL ARR 2026 submission · Prof. Ben Hu · Rice University
  • Contributed to a controlled, MAS-demanding benchmark re-evaluating five pruning methods (AgentPrune, AgentDropout, AGP, DAGP, GPTSwarm) under a shared model, agent, and runtime contract.
  • Cost and structural pipelines show that many reported efficiency gains stem from graph collapse or easy pruning rather than genuine compression.
May 2025 — Aug 2025 Beijing, China Applied ML

ML-Driven Virtual Metrology in Semiconductor Fabrication

Integration, the VLSI Journal (2026) · Prof. Wenjian Yu · Tsinghua University
  • Built an ML virtual-metrology framework predicting HDPCVD dielectric thin-film thickness across 14/55/130 nm nodes directly from production logs (recipes, runsheets, metrology).
  • Engineered the data-curation and feature pipeline (recipe-based imputation, IQR outlier removal, near-zero-variance filtering) and implemented CNN, RNN, MLP, and XGBoost models.
  • Introduced a specification-aware metric (ACC@X) aligning evaluation with fab tolerances; XGBoost reached R² = 0.92 and 79.5% within-tolerance accuracy at 14 nm.
